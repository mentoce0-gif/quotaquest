# POSITIONING.md — QuotaQuest

> Phase 2 deliverable. How we are different, who we are for, and the one sentence that sells it.

---

## 1. One-liner

**QuotaQuest turns your AI quota into an MMORPG status bar.**
HP, MP, EXP and a recovery timer — always on top, half-transparent, on your desktop.

> "Stop reading `/usage`. Start watching your HP."

---

## 2. The problem (in the user's words)

- "I never know how much Claude I have left until I suddenly hit the wall mid-task."
- "Two limits — a 5-hour window AND a weekly cap — and I can't feel either of them."
- "I check `/usage` ten times a day. It's an anxiety tic."
- "Existing monitors are accurate but boring. They feel like another chore."

The pain is **real-time situational awareness without context-switching** — and doing it in
a way that's *enjoyable* instead of stressful.

## 4. Differentiation — 5 wedges

1. **Emotion, not just data.** Competitors reduce anxiety by showing numbers. We *transform*
   the anxiety into a game you want to look at. NORMAL → WARNING → CRITICAL is a vibe, not a metric.
2. **MMORPG metaphor that already lives in users' heads.** HP = weekly health, MP = session
   mana, EXP = today's grind, Recovery Timer = potion cooldown, Burn Rate = DPS. Zero learning curve.
3. **Rainmeter-native = a free distribution channel.** We reach the millions in the
   Rainmeter / Wallpaper Engine / r/desktops scene that *no* AI-usage tool currently touches.
   Skinnable, themeable, remixable — the community markets us.
4. **Built to be screenshotted.** "My Claude HP is at 8%, CRITICAL 🩸" is a tweet. Dashboards
   don't go viral; RPG health bars do. Virality is designed in.
5. **Streamer / vibe-coder native.** Half-transparent + always-on-top = drop-in OBS overlay.
   We're content, not just a tool.

## 6. Target segments (priority order)

2. **Vibe Coders / indie hackers** — identity-driven, love aesthetic dev setups, share online.
3. **AI-tool streamers** — want overlays; turn us into broadcast furniture = organic reach.
4. **Cursor / Copilot / Windsurf users** — expansion once we add their adapters.
5. **Rainmeter / desktop-customization hobbyists** — distribution multiplier, not just users.

---

## 7. Messaging pillars

- **Glance, don't check.** Your quota, ambient and always visible — no command, no tab-switch.
- **Feel your limits.** Color and status make "I'm running low" a feeling, not a calculation.
- **Your desktop, your character sheet.** Skinnable RPG HUD that fits a setup you're proud of.
- **Lightweight & local.** Reads local logs, writes a tiny JSON, sips resources. No account, no cloud.

---

## 8. Competitive responses & our moat

- *They add a theme.* → We have first-mover brand + the deepest Rainmeter skin library + a community.
- *Anthropic ships official usage UI.* → It'll be a settings page, not an MMORPG overlay; we
  complement it. We compete on delight and desktop presence, not raw numbers.
- *Someone clones the idea.* → Moat = brand ("QuotaQuest" = the category name), skin ecosystem,
  multi-tool adapters, and the meme/streamer footprint we build first.
